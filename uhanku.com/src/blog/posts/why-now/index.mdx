## MYSELF (PROFESSIONAL OVERTHINKER)

<DraftCard title="Should we?">
Anyone who knows me knows that I usually work on my personal ideas and projects quietly, alone, without anybody to judge me. That's how I've always liked it.

I rarely talk about what I'm building or show it to other people, but not because I don't like it, but because whenever I do it, I tend to feel the need to make the best possible impression.

And making the best impression, for me, often takes way too much energy.

[QUESTION: Can someone not technical use it?]
I feel the project can't just work. It needs to look like a proper MVP. The project should be intuitive, easy to understand, simple and most of it should look intentional.

After handling the MVP, often I wouldn't, but, with it done, overthinking phase comes.

[QUESTION: Who am I showing this project to?]
How should I introduce the idea?
By saying this am I being too technical?
Is this way of explaining actually good?
Should I rebuild the whole thing before anyone sees it?
Should I simply disappear and pretend I never built it?

This resumes the kind of thinking I do after the MVP and during the MVP, I don't know I do that sometimes, but apparently my brain believes every side project needs the communication strategy of a multinational company.

I hate that part.

It's stressful, anxious work. No, brain, listen! From now on we are going to reserve this kind of overthinking for the actual job.

My personal projects are supposed to be where curiosity gets to run wild, not my anxiety!

</DraftCard>

## Paying to work

<DraftCard title="Should we?">

Recently I spoke to many SWE and many of them are also in the same state I'm currently in, we know how AI writes code faster, we know there is no competing against that, the funny part is nobody is with a gun in our heads forcing us to use AI... We use and pay for it because of all the things it can do and the hype behind it too.

The thing is, we know if we don't use it we will feel left behind, in the current market everybody just knows how to talk about that. The layoffs, the marketing, the hype. Not only those things happening globally make us use it, but we have this feeling of, "ok, it does amazing things, it can do things faster than I do, I don't know how it works, if I stop using it something crazy might happen in the future and I'll miss it, so what do I need to get up to date with this thing and not be left behind?"

</DraftCard>
